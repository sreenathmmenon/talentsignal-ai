## You're not short on candidates. You're short on the 32% a keyword search hides from you — and the hours to find them.

**Angle:** The overworked recruiter / HR leader — TalentSignal finds the people they're missing, saves them hours, and lets them defend every decision.

Every recruiter knows the quiet failure of the modern pipeline: the perfect person is in the stack, but they wrote "built distributed systems" where the JD said "microservices," so the ATS never surfaced them. On our real 100,000-candidate pool, 32% of the top-100 were people a keyword search would have missed entirely — rescued by meaning, not spelling. TalentSignal ranks any résumé against any JD by what the words mean (embeddings plus lexical), so the strong candidate who used different vocabulary stops falling through the floor.

But finding them is only half the job — the other half is trusting and defending the shortlist. TalentSignal doesn't hand you a mystery score. Every candidate arrives with a verdict (Strong / Worth-a-look / Weak), a Matched✓ / Missing✗ skills panel that shows exactly what lined up and what didn't, a "found by meaning" flag when the match came from semantics rather than keywords, and a reachability tag (reachable / passive / stale). You stop reading résumés to build a shortlist; you read a shortlist to make a decision. In the 100K proof run, that shortlist reproduces byte-identically offline in about 70 seconds — no GPU, no per-token bill, no data leaving the box.

The reachability model was designed with an HR council, and it corrects the single most expensive mistake in sourcing: throwing away everyone whose "open to work" toggle is off. On the real pool, 64% are not-open — but 22% of those are still reachable. So availability re-ranks, it never filters. The strong passive senior you'd never have messaged now surfaces near the top with a "passive" tag, instead of being invisibly deleted before you ever saw the name.

And when a decision gets questioned — by a candidate, a hiring manager, or legal — you're not defending a black box. TalentSignal ships a candidate-facing transparency report (what matched, with proof, and what to improve — a humane answer instead of a silent rejection), a role-independent consistency auditor that flags impossible or honeypot profiles by contradiction rather than keywords (0 fabricated profiles made our top-100, and 100% résumé-attack resistance on the semantic engine vs 78% on the keyword fallback), and an EEOC four-fifths adverse-impact check you can run against your own group labels. You can explain every ranking, to anyone, on demand.

TalentSignal surfaces a Strong-verdict candidate flagged 'found by meaning' — she wrote 'decomposed the monolith into independently deployable services,' which the ATS scored as zero. Her Matched✓ panel confirms the fit; a 'passive' reachability tag tells the recruiter she isn't actively looking but is reachable. One targeted message instead of forty cold reads.
- **Defending the shortlist in the debrief** (An HR leader challenged by a hiring manager on why candidate #7 ranked below #3): Instead of relitigating gut feel, she opens each candidate's verdict and Matched✓/Missing✗ panel side by side in compare view. #3's Missing✗ list is shorter and his matched skills carry proof. Meanwhile the consistency auditor had already quietly caught a fabricated-tenure honeypot before it wasted an interview slot — flagged by contradiction, not by a keyword blocklist.

_Differentiator:_ A keyword ATS misses 32% of your best people by vocabulary and hands you a score you can't defend. An LLM screener will confidently rank a gamed résumé — the exact attacks TalentSignal blocks 100% of on the semantic engine — and costs per token with no reproducibility.
