# Candidate Archetypes

## Strong AI/Search/Ranking Engineer

Typical signals:

- Relevant current/recent title.
- Production retrieval, ranking, search, recommendation, embeddings, or vector infrastructure evidence.
- Strong Python/ML systems skills.
- Good behavioral availability.

Ranking treatment:

- Top-10 eligible when evidence is direct and logistics are acceptable.

## Adjacent Backend/Data Engineer

Typical signals:

- Backend/data title.
- Career history mentions search, data pipelines, ML infrastructure, retrieval, or evaluation.
- May not have exact AI Engineer title.

Ranking treatment:

- Top-50 candidate if production evidence is strong.

## Product ML Generalist

Typical signals:

- ML/data science title.
- Shipped models or user-facing ML features.
- Some retrieval/ranking/evaluation evidence.

Ranking treatment:

- Strong if production and product signals exist.

## Pure Research Candidate

Typical signals:

- Research title or academic language.
- Papers/models but no deployed systems.

Ranking treatment:

- Down-rank unless production deployment evidence is present.

## AI Keyword Stuffer

Typical signals:

- Many AI skills.
- Unrelated current title.
- No supporting career history.

Ranking treatment:

- Penalize strongly, not top-100 unless other evidence exists.

## Stale Strong Candidate

Typical signals:

- Excellent static fit.
- Not active, low response, slow response, not open to work.

Ranking treatment:

- Down-rank relative to active comparable candidates.

## Service-Only Candidate

Typical signals:

- Career only at service/consulting firms.
- No product-company or deployed product ownership.

Ranking treatment:

- Penalize unless profile has strong production/retrieval evidence.
